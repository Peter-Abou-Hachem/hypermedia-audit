Hypermedia operates one of the largest in-store retail media networks in the UAE. The technology powering the smart screens — audience detection, campaign delivery, analytics, and reporting — is currently provided by AiOO Technologies. This assessment examines what AiOO provides, how dependent Hypermedia is on that technology, whether it can be replicated, and at what cost.

The goal: full technology independence — Hypermedia owns its own platform, its own data, and its own roadmap. No vendor lock-in. No revenue share to a third party. No single point of failure.

Architecture: Two Software Layers

Understanding which layer AiOO controls is critical for planning the transition.

Layer 1 — On the Device (Edge)

Software running ON the sensor/player hardware. Captures raw sensor data, processes it into structured audience data (age, gender, dwell time, attention level).

Key question: Does this software come pre-installed with the hardware, or does AiOO install it? This determines whether the devices can function independently.
Layer 2 — Cloud Platform

Everything that receives the structured data and runs the business: campaign management, programmatic exchange, ops workflows, device monitoring, attribution, dashboards and reporting.

This is AiOO's platform. Campaign IQ, AiOO X, Flow, Raidar, Echo, and the Hypermedia-branded dashboard are all hosted and operated by AiOO.

04 · AiOO Claims — Independent Validation

What they say vs. what holds up.

Every claim below is sourced from AiOO's own documents and evaluated independently.

"No direct competitor has assembled the full stack"

AiOO positions itself as the only platform combining sensors + ad server + SSP + attribution in one stack.

Source: AiOO GTM Strategy, "Competitive Positioning" slide
PARTIALLY TRUE

Assessment: Individual components all have established competitors. Advertima and Quividi offer audience measurement. Broadsign, Vistar Media, and Hivestack offer DOOH ad serving and SSP capabilities. What is genuinely uncommon is the full vertical integration — sensor to attribution in one platform from one vendor. However, "no competitor" overstates the case. The correct framing is: no single competitor bundles all five layers, but each layer has mature alternatives that can be integrated.

"$5-10M and 36-48 months to build in-house"

AiOO's suggested cost for a retailer to replicate the platform independently.

Source: AiOO GTM Strategy, "Competitive Positioning" slide, footer note
INFLATED

Assessment: This figure assumes building the FULL stack from scratch: custom sensors, ad server, SSP with DSP integrations, attribution with Body ID, multi-retailer network architecture, and a multi-year go-to-market. For Hypermedia's operational needs — campaign management, audience intelligence, dashboards, and device monitoring — the realistic rebuild cost is $150-250K over 3-4 months. Advanced features (SSP, attribution) can be added incrementally. The $5-10M figure is designed to discourage the exact conversation we are having.

"Patented real-time audience triggering"

AiOO claims their audience-triggered ad delivery technology is patented.

Source: AiOO GTM Strategy, "Competitive Positioning" slide — "YES (patented)"
UNVERIFIED

Assessment: No patent number is provided in any of the seven documents reviewed. "Patented" may refer to a patent application (pending), not a granted patent. Real-time audience-triggered content delivery is a well-established technique in the DOOH industry (Quividi, Advertima, and others have offered variations since 2018+). Recommendation: request the patent number and verify scope before this claim influences any technical or legal decisions.

"Privacy-by-design: no PII, no facial recognition, no cloud storage of raw video"

All processing on-device. Only anonymized, aggregated analytics leave the device.

Source: AiOO Short Intro, "AudienceEye" + "Privacy & Compliance" slides
CREDIBLE

Assessment: Edge processing with no cloud video transmission is a legitimate and increasingly standard architecture pattern. This is how Advertima, Quividi, and other mature competitors also operate. The claim is credible and should be maintained in any replacement platform. However: Echo's "Body ID" re-identification (tracking people across a store by body shape and clothing) is a gray area — while technically not facial recognition, it does create a re-identifiable signature that regulators may scrutinize under GDPR's definition of personal data.

"3.2x attention lift"

AiOO claims audience-targeted campaigns achieve 3.2x more attention than standard loop-based DOOH.

Source: AiOO Short Intro, "From Static Screens to Intelligent Media" slide
UNSUBSTANTIATED

Assessment: No methodology, sample size, control group definition, or measurement period is provided. "Attention lift" is not a standardized industry metric. Competing claims in DOOH typically range from 1.5-4x depending on measurement methodology. Without transparent methodology, this number cannot be validated and should not be used in brand-facing reporting without independent verification.

"$45/screen/month is fair vs. market benchmarks"

AiOO positions the fee as "monetization infrastructure, not screen scheduling software."

Source: 2026 AiOO Commercial Terms document
CONTEXT-DEPENDENT

Assessment: AiOO's own benchmark table shows basic CMS at $10-20/screen/month and enterprise at $30-50+. The $45 fee is within range for a full ad server + audience intelligence stack. However, this is the introductory rate — their full stack pricing (Pulse + Connect + Echo) reaches $160-230/screen/month. At scale:

At $45 × TBC screens$81K-$180K/month$972K-$2.16M/year
Full stack pricing$288K-$920K/month$3.5-$11M/year

The COFREE model (alternative) results in AiOO taking ~60% effective take-rate on Hypermedia's screen revenue. On $3M/year revenue, that is $1.8M/year going to AiOO.

Source: AiOO GTM Strategy, "Revenue Architecture" + "COFREE Model" slides

"Closed-loop POS attribution — deterministic, not modelled"

Echo claims to match audience exposure directly to point-of-sale transactions.

Source: AiOO GTM Strategy, "Competitive Positioning" + AiOO Short Intro, "AiOO Echo" slide
TECHNICALLY POSSIBLE BUT COMPLEX

Assessment: Body ID re-identification (tracking a person from screen exposure to checkout) is real computer vision research being deployed by a small number of companies. However: (a) accuracy degrades significantly in crowded retail environments, (b) matching requires sensor coverage at both screen AND checkout, (c) the "deterministic" claim implies 1:1 matching which is aspirational — most implementations are probabilistic. This is the most technically advanced and hardest-to-replicate component of AiOO's stack. It is also the feature Hypermedia can most easily live without in the short term — brands have accepted modelled attribution for decades.

"Player agnostic — works with any hardware"

AiOO claims modular architecture that integrates with existing CMS and hardware ecosystems.

Source: AiOO Short Intro, "Integrations" slide
PARTIALLY TRUE

Assessment: Their documentation specifies "Android-based and edge-player friendly architecture." This suggests compatibility with standard Android media players, which is good. However, the AudienceEye edge processing requires specific compute capability (GPU for CV inference) that not all standard players have. The claim is true for the cloud platform (Campaign IQ, Flow, Raidar) but likely requires specific hardware for the audience intelligence layer. This is positive for a rebuild — if the players are standard Android devices, a replacement platform can be deployed on the same hardware.

06 · Risk Assessment

What needs to be confirmed
before committing to the rebuild.

Risk 1 · Edge Software Dependency

Can the sensor hardware function without AiOO's software?

If AiOO installs proprietary edge software that locks the sensor output, the devices cannot produce audience data without their platform running. This would require a full edge CV rebuild — adding 4-8 weeks and requiring a specialized computer vision engineer.

Status: Pending confirmation from Hypermedia technical team
Risk 2 · Data Portability

Where does historical campaign and audience data live?

If all data is stored on AiOO's cloud infrastructure and there is no contractual obligation to provide data export, Hypermedia risks losing all historical benchmarks, campaign performance records, and audience analytics. This weakens brand renewal conversations.

Status: Pending confirmation
Risk 3 · Patent Claim

AiOO claims "patented" audience triggering

No patent number provided. Must be verified. If a granted patent exists with broad scope, it could constrain how any replacement platform implements real-time audience-triggered ad delivery. Most DOOH patents are narrow in scope.

Action: Request patent number from AiOO. Conduct independent patent search.
Risk 4 · Transition Continuity

What happens to live campaigns during migration?

Active brand campaigns must continue running during the transition. A parallel-run period is required — both platforms operating simultaneously until the new platform is validated. Requires coordination with AiOO during the transition window.

Mitigation: 2-4 week parallel run before full cutover

08 · Two Paths Forward

Full replacement or phased control.
Both lead to independence.

There are two approaches to achieving technology independence. The right choice depends on the answers to the technical questions in Section 9 — particularly sensor access and data portability.

Option A — Control Layer First

Own the decisioning. Swap components over time.

Build a thin orchestration layer that controls which ads run on which screens and when. This is where revenue flows — owning this layer means owning the margin. Keep existing sensor hardware and AiOO's edge software running initially. Replace components incrementally as the team validates each layer.

Investment$40-80K
Timeline6 weeks
DeliversAd decisioning + dashboard + reporting
DefersEdge CV rebuild, SSP, attribution
Risk levelLow — minimal disruption to live ops
Best if: Sensor hardware can continue running independently; immediate priority is margin control and dashboard ownership; lower upfront investment preferred.
Option B — Full Platform Replacement

Replace the entire AiOO stack. Full ownership from day one.

Build a complete replacement: campaign management, audience intelligence (edge CV), dashboards, device monitoring, and data migration. Clean break from AiOO. Hypermedia owns every layer of the stack. Parallel run with existing platform before cutover to ensure zero disruption.

Investment$150-250K
Timeline3 months
DeliversFull platform: ad server + CV + dashboards + monitoring
DefersSSP (programmatic), attribution (POS matching)
Risk levelMedium — requires parallel run period
Best if: Edge software is AiOO-dependent (Layer 1 answer); clean break is the priority; Hypermedia wants to fully own the audience intelligence layer.
Note: Both options lead to the same place — full technology independence. Option A gets there incrementally with less risk. Option B gets there in one move with more upfront investment. The answers from the technical team (especially questions #2 and #4) will determine which is the right fit.
09 · What the Transition Looks Like

Week by week.
No surprises. No gaps.

Week 1 — Technical Audit
  • Day 1-2: On-site technical audit of one representative store — sensor hardware, device specs, data output format, network configuration
  • Day 3: Scope confirmation based on findings. Option A or B determined.
  • Day 4-5: Finalized statement of work with milestones and deliverables
  • Outcome: Full clarity on what we're working with, what needs to be built, and exactly how long it takes
  • Parallel: No disruption to current AiOO operations. Everything continues running as-is during audit.
Weeks 2-4 — Build & Test
  • Week 2: First dashboard prototype connected to real Hypermedia data. Team reviews UX.
  • Week 3: Campaign management core operational. First test campaigns running on 5-10 screens in one store.
  • Week 4: Edge pipeline validated (if Option B). Device monitoring live across test cluster.
Weeks 5-8 — Expand & Validate
  • Week 5-6: Platform expanded to 50-100 screens. Real brand campaigns running in parallel with AiOO.
  • Week 7-8: Full reporting validated. Brand reports match or exceed current quality. Ops team trained on new platform.
Weeks 9-12 — Cutover (Option B only)
  • Week 9-10: Parallel run — both platforms operating simultaneously across the full network. No single point of failure.
  • Week 11-12: Cutover to new platform. AiOO services decommissioned. Hypermedia operates independently.
Key principle: The current platform continues running untouched throughout the transition. No screen goes dark. No campaign is interrupted. No brand notices anything. The new platform is validated in parallel before any switch happens.

Improved
